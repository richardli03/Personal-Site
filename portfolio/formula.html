Olin Electric Motorsports (OEM) builds an electric Formula-style racing vehicle every year to compete at a Formula SAE competition in June. Our team is composed of ~50 engineers, who are responsible for everything from chassis design and fabrication, to PCB design and electrical harnessing.


As a pure software engineer, I wasn't sure I'd have a place on the team when I first joined in 2021. However, I gradually noticed that there were was an opportunity use software as a tool to collect, display, and utilize data to improve our car. I started off working on creating a Real Time Data Visualization system and now I'm the Head of Sensing and Modeling on the team.

Real Time Data Visualization

My first project on the team was creating a real time data visualization system that was capable of taking data transmitted from the car and displaying it to our engineers. The codebase uses Python for data cleaning/analysis, Redis for data streaming, Graphana for data visualization, and Docker for architecture. One of our key goals was to use as much open-source plug-and-play software as possible and create as many tools as possible ourselves. We were very conscious of the fact that this would not be the final product for the team, and wanted the flexibility of improving/changing the tools we used where possible. with our codebase, we could stream real data from the car and produce something like the graph to the right.

Head of Sensing and Modeling

When I attended competition last June, one of the deepest insights I got was during the design review. The judges kept on asking why we chose to make various design decisions, and although we had all of datasheets to back up our viewpoints, we couldn't produce the simulations or data necessary to ensure that we had made the right choice. Having created the data visualization system that was to go on the car next year, I realized that this was a niche that we should be trying hard to fill, and I proposed the creation of a sensing and modeling team. Now, I'm responsible for 7 engineers who are working on integrating various software tools and sensors onto the vehicle to inform our future design decisions and give us unprecendented information about the way our car drives.



Aside from sensing and modeling specific responsibilities, I also work with the other leads on the team to make design decisions about the current iteration of our car, including harnessing and board placement. We are also responsible for onboarding the nearly 10 new members that join Formula each year and giving them introductions to electrical design software, circuit/schematic construction, and software design.
